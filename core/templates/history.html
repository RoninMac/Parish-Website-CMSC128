Our Parish History

The history of the Virgen de la Candelaria Parish can be traced back to 1958, when Tranquilino Eusebio and Clara Mendiolia donated a 275-square-meter lot in Bagacay for the construction of a community chapel. The first chapel was built by Pedro C. Buclatan and later maintained by Alberto Compas. This early chapel was originally dedicated to Saint Roch of Montpellier (San Roque), reflecting the strong devotion of the barangay to its long-time patron.

For several decades, the chapel served as the center of faith for families in Bagacay and nearby sitios. As the Catholic population grew, pastoral needs expanded beyond what the simple wooden chapel could support.

On December 7, 2005, the chapel was officially elevated to a mission station under the Archdiocese of Palo and given the title Virgen de la Candelaria Mission Station. This marked the beginning of regular pastoral assignments, formal record-keeping, and a more organized sacramental ministry. Our Lady of the Candles became the missionâ€™s primary patroness, while Saint Roch remained the secondary patron of the barangay.

The mission station faced a major challenge when Super Typhoon Yolanda (Haiyan) struck on November 8, 2013, causing severe damage to the chapel and surrounding areas. Community members, with the support of the Archdiocese, began efforts to rebuild and expand the church to better serve the growing population of Bagacay.

After years of ongoing development, the mission achieved a significant milestone on June 28, 2022, when it was officially erected as a full-fledged parish: the Virgen de la Candelaria Parish of Bagacay, Tacloban City. Today, the parish oversees multiple chapels across several barangays, serving as a spiritual home and center of pastoral activity for thousands of parishioners.

Our Mission

Guided by Our Lady of the Candles and Saint Roch, the parish is committed to fostering a vibrant, welcoming, and faith-filled community. Through evangelization, service, education, and the celebration of the sacraments, the parish continues to uphold its mission of spiritual growth and community-building for generations to come.
